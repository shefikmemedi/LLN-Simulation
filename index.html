V. Simulate the Law of Large Numbers (LLN) by plotting multiple (m) trajectories of (n) trials for the relative frequency f(n). Show with a histogram (possibly, a vertically oriented histogram placed on the right side of the main chart) how, as soon as n becomes large, the empirical distribution of f(n) (over all m trajectories) tends to the underlying probability p of success used in each trial.

Exploring the Law of Large Numbers in Action

Watch how the relative frequency stabilizes near the true probability as the number of trials grows.

Simple Explanation

Each line represents one simulated sequence of outcomes where an event with probability p occurs repeatedly. As the number of trials increases, the average frequency of successes (blue lines) begins to cluster around the true probability (gold line). This demonstrates the Law of Large Numbers, which shows that with enough observations, the experimental probability approaches the theoretical one.
